bento•lab

bento•lab is a personal box-sized biological laboratory. It is equipped with a powerful 3d-printed centrifuge, a mini PCR machine, and a gel electrophoresis unit with an LED transilluminator. Everything fits in a portable A4-sized box.

As part of the team developing the bento•lab, I was responsible for bento's brand identity, user experience design, product design, as well as some elements of strategy and planning. Currently, the bento•lab is in pioneer beta-testing.

Visual Design for Data Explorers

Data scientists create new tools for exploring and making sense of huge sets of data generated by data-driven sciences (for example genomics or population health). Visualisation is often a key component of those tools. Yet, many data scientists lack important design skills to make good visualisation choices.

I created a half-day hands-on workshop on the principles of visual design for data scientists. I taught it at two IEEEVis conferences in 2012 (Seattle, WA) and 2013 (Atlanta, GA). The workshop includes a 90-minute background talk followed by a 120-minute sketching exercise, and a 30-minute discussion.

Human Factors

Human factors (or ergonomics) is the study of interactions between people and the things they use. It's possible to design healthy, comfortable, efficient objects and environments only if human factors are well considered.

Same with designing entire workspaces. In 2006 I conducted a human factors evaluation of a conference room for a pharma company based in Warsaw. I analysed key conflicts between users, architecture, and office equipment, and made a list of recommendations for improvements. The recommendations were aimed at significantly improving human factors quality of the conference room, while keeping the extent of changes to minimum.

Lingua Extraterrestris

Are we alone in the Universe?

"Lingua Extraterrestris" is my MA thesis and an exploration into the limits of communication. I'm interested in CETI — communication with extarterrestrial intelligence — because it is the most extreme example of all communication types. My investigation looks at historical and science-fiction examples of CETI, and discusses them in relation to prerequisites for any communication encounter: channel, contact, context, and code. In conclusion, I suggest to expand on the famous Drake equation using these four parameters.

{% endif %} {% if page.label == "mitosis" %}

Mitosis and Meiosis

"What is the most complex thing in biology that students have trouble understanding"? I asked this question to my former high-school biology teacher, Dr Włodzimierz Wójcik, when we started a collaboration project to redesign visualisation aids he used in his class. "Mitosis and meiosis" — was his immediate answer.

Mitosis and meiosis are two types of cell division that underpin entire biology. I created three diagrams explaining mitosis, meiosis, and the relationship between them. In 2008 I released the diagrams under the Creative Commons CC BY-SA 3.0 license. Since then, they have been adopted by 22 Wikipedia pages in 12 languages, used in teaching in Poland and the USA, and published in books. They also come up in the first line of Google image search for "mitosis" and "meiosis".

Paradoxes

A paradox is a seemingly self-contradicing statement, that yet can be true. There are many paradoxes in philosophy and sciences, for example: the Liar Paradox, the Arrow Paradox, the Twin Paradox, or the Fermi Paradox.

"Paradoxes" is a workshop I authored and led during the Thinking in Visual Communication conference in Warsaw in 2014. Workshop task was to use visualisation to explain a selected paradox, and later to propose a speculative visual solution to that paradox. Workshop's focus was on exploring visual strategies for communicating highly complex and abstract concepts.

{% endif %} {% if page.label == "bundles" %}

Sequence Bundles

Sequence Bundles is a new visualisation method for discovering and exploring sequence motifs in multiple sequence alignment data. It can be used to find and investigate patterns, correlations and variation in protein or genetic data.

Sequence Bundles were designed at Science Practice. I initiated, managed and directed the project, establishing a collaboration with the Goldman group at the EMBL-EBI, winning Innovate UK funding for the development of an interactive bioinformatics tool, and co-authoring peer-reviewed academic publications.
