The bento•lab houses a powerful centrifuge, a mini PCR machine, and gel electrophoresis unit with blue LED transillumination. Everything fits in a portable A4-sized dibond box.

When used in regular biotech labs, the bento•lab can serve as a compact, personal prototyping "sandbox" for testing out hypotheses and conducting draft experiments.

bento•lab is portable, so it can be used to do basic lab work in almost any given setting, for instance at home, in the office, and even outdoors.

In 2013 I joined the UCL iGEM Entrepeneurship team to advise them on their project: a portable lab-in-the-box for biology. Initially, we called it Darwin Toolbox. The first version of our Darwin Toolbox was enclosed inside an off-the-shelve suitcase for good protection and portability. For later design iterations we considered building a bespoke casing for the lab.
