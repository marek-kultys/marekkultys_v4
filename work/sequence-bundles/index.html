We created Sequence Bundles to help bioinformaticians discover and explore sequence motifs in their protein or DNA Multiple Sequence Alignments.

We created Sequence Bundles to help bioinformaticians discover and explore sequence motifs in their protein or DNA Multiple Sequence Alignments.

The Sequence Bundles plot is a variation on parallel coordinates. The X-axis shows all sequence positions (here 36). The Y-axis lists all amino acids in a protein sequence (here 20). Each sequence is represented with a line. Lines are continuous and semi-opaque, so it is easy to see as they converge in regions of consensus, and diverge in areas of variability.

Sequence Bundles is available online as a visualisation web-app. We developed it at Science Practice to enable quick and easy visualisation of protein data. Images generated with the web-app can be used for publications.

Sequence Bundles have also been implemented in Alvis — an open-source desktop platform for data exploration and analysis in bioinformatics. We collaborated on developing Sequence Bundles and Alvis with the Goldman group at the European Bioinformatics Institute in Hinxton, UK.
