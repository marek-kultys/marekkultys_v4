As part of the team developing the bento•lab, I was responsible for bento's identity, product design, and user experience, as well as related elements of strategy and planning. bento•lab is currenty in pioneer beta-testing.

Human Factors

Human factors (or ergonomics) is the study of interactions between people and the things they use. It's possible to design healthy, comfortable, efficient objects and environments only if human factors are well considered.

Same with designing entire workspaces. In 2006 I conducted a human factors evaluation of a conference room for a pharma company based in Warsaw. I analysed key conflicts between users, architecture, and office equipment, and made a list of recommendations for improvements. The recommendations were aimed at significantly improving human factors quality of the conference room, while keeping the extent of changes to minimum.

Lingua Extraterrestris

Are we alone in the Universe?

Lingua Extraterrestris is my MA thesis and an exploration into the limits of communication. I'm interested in CETI — Communication with ExtraTerrestrial Intelligence — because it is the most extreme example of all communication types. My investigation looks into historical and science-fiction examples of CETI and concludes in expanding on the seminal Drake Equation.

I presented Lingua Extraterrestris at two astrobiology conferences and in a workshop at the SETI Institute in California.

{% endif %} {% if page.label == "mitosis" %}

Mitosis and Meiosis

"What is the most complex thing in biology that students have trouble understanding"? I asked this question to my former high-school biology teacher, Dr Włodzimierz Wójcik, when we started a collaboration project to redesign visualisation aids he used in his class. "Mitosis and meiosis" — was his immediate answer.

Mitosis and meiosis are two types of cell division that underpin entire biology. I created three diagrams explaining mitosis, meiosis, and the relationship between them. In 2008 I released the diagrams under the Creative Commons CC BY-SA 3.0 license. Since then, they have been adopted by 22 Wikipedia pages in 12 languages, used in teaching in Poland and the USA, and published in books. They also come up in the first line of Google image search for "mitosis" and "meiosis".

{% endif %} {% if page.label == "bundles" %}

Sequence Bundles

Sequence Bundles is a new visualisation method for discovering and exploring sequence motifs in multiple sequence alignment data. It can be used to find and investigate patterns, correlations and variation in protein or genetic data.

Sequence Bundles were designed at Science Practice. I initiated, managed and directed the project, establishing a collaboration with the Goldman group at the EMBL-EBI, winning Innovate UK funding for the development of an interactive bioinformatics tool, and co-authoring peer-reviewed academic publications.
