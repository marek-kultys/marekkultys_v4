Visual Design for Data Explorers

Data scientists create new tools for exploring and making sense of huge sets of data generated by data-driven sciences (for example genomics or population health). Visualisation is often a key component of those tools. Yet, many data scientists lack important design skills to make good visualisation choices.

I created a half-day hands-on workshop on the principles of visual design for data scientists. I taught it at two IEEEVis conferences in 2012 (Seattle, WA) and 2013 (Atlanta, GA). The workshop includes a 90-minute background talk followed by a 120-minute sketching exercise, and a 30-minute discussion.
