In mitosis, a cell simply makes an exact copy of itself (a single somatic diploid cell divides into two identical diploid cells).
CC BY-SA

In meiosis, a cell splits into four reproductive cells — in humans it's eggs or sperm (a single diploid cell divides into four haploid gametes or meiospores).
CC BY-SA

Mitosis allows multicellular organisms to grow, build tissues, and replace worn out cells. Meiosis enables sexual reproduction and adds some genetic variation to each new generation of organisms.
CC BY-SA
