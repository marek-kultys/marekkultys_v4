We created Sequence Bundles to help bioinformaticians see hidden correlations and sequence motifs in their Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA) data.

We created Sequence Bundles to help bioinformaticians see hidden correlations and sequence motifs in their Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA) data.

The X-axis shows all positions in the alignment (here 36). The Y-axis lists all amino acids in a protein sequence (here 21) and gaps. Each line represents an individual sequence. Lines are continuous and semi-opaque, so it is easy to see as they converge in regions of consensus, and diverge in areas of variability.

At Science Practice we developed the Sequence Bundles web-app. It is a free tool for quick and easy visualisation of protein data, available for bioscience users for research and publications.
